Storm Surge: Hazards and Preparedness

Hazards from Storm Surges

It is essential that people know the hazards that are around their environment in order to prepare, respond and recover from it - the primary hazards during a storm surge are:

  • Flooding along the coast, severely eroded beaches, and coastal roads
  • buildings collapsing as a result of eroded foundations
  • salt water intrusion in fresh water wells (pumping of water may be suspended)
  • temporary migration of animals from coastal areas to urban areas due to salt water in their habitat.

Color coded warnings
Storm surge warnings can be found in the PAGASA Severe Weather Bulletin and in their impact-based color-coded storm surge warnings. It is stated there: all residents living in low areas of the seashore are advised to move to more elevated areas and keep in mind all reminders and warnings on floods brought by the storm surge. Mitigation Plan
Keep in mind as individuals:

  1. Follow weather updates from PAGASA and other assisting government agencies via the radio, television, or internet.
  2. Be ready for the possibility of evacuating to a more elevated area or evacuation center even before the storm surge happens. Prepare and bring the following with you: clothes, food and water, first aid kit, flashlight, and battery-operated radio.
  3. Before evacuating, search the house and fix its weak parts. Tightly close the windows and turn off the electrical main switch. Place your important belongings to a high place.



Evacuation Plan

  1. BEFORE
  • Know your local emergency hotlines.
  • Prepare your emergency survival kit.
  • Monitor weather conditions.
  • Strengthen your house.
  • Evacuate if necessary.

  1. DURING
  • Stay indoors.
  • Stay away from floods and coastal areas.
  • Gather family members and stay calm.
  • Stay in the highest portion of your house, away from windows.

  1. AFTER
  • Drink and eat food prepared with clean water.
  • Thoroughly check your home.
  • Participate in clean-up activities.
  • Take photos of properties covered by insurance.
